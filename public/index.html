Time $pent

Time $pent is an app that was designed to help users see their time through a different perspective. Instead of seeing time as a rigid chronological system of hours, minutes, and seconds, users can gauge how much time they really have in a similar way to how they plan their budget while looking at their bank account. Instead of planning out what will happen at some specific time in the day, users can budget what they "spend" their time on, and keep track of their "budget" of time throughout the day. Your time budget refers to the 1,440 minutes in each day available to you. Many people either overestimate or underestimate how much time they have to accomplish what they want for the day and seeing time as more of a finite resource can help people more effectively go about their daily tasks.

One obvious difference between time and money is that time is "spent" regardless of what you choose to do with it. Everyday your budget of minutes will fall all the way to 0 even if you do absolutely nothing with your time. Knowing this can help users make better choices about what to spend their time on. If everyone had a daily budget of $1,440 in real money, and this budget would disapear by the end of the day, just about everyone would figure out something worthwhile to do with all that money. Seeing time from this perspective might motivate people to conciously spend their time rather than just letting it slip away as the day goes on.
