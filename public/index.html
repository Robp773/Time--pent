Time $pent

Time $pent is an app that was designed to help users see their time through a different perspective. Instead of seeing time as a rigid chronological system of hours, minutes, and seconds, users can gauge how much time they really have in a similar way to how they plan their budget while looking at their bank account. Instead of planning out what will happen at some specific time in the day, users can budget what they "spend" their time on, and keep track of their "budget" of time throughout the day.

Key terms to help make sense of the app...

Daily Budget- refers to how many minutes you have to make use of in one day.

(24 hours x 60 minutes = 1,440)

Records- an event which has already taken place that you want to log. For example, if you wake up at 7:30 AM, you could immediately log an item called "sleep" alongwith the amount of time it took in minutes (probably around 400 minutes) and other descriptive details. The amount of time it took will be subtracted from your daily budget and the item and the other details you logged will be added to the records column.

To Do's- work as you would expect-events that have not yet happened that you want to eventually accomplish before the end of the day. Adding a new To Do works the same way as adding a record except that the amount of time required for the To Do will be subtracted from the number of minutes left in the day.

Budget Summary- the middle column that does all of the math for the app. Basically 1,440 minus your recorded minutes minus the time that has passed that was not recorded equals the amount of time left in the day or "Current Budget". Using the Current Budget, we subtract the cost of your planned To Dos to get the final number of minutes which represents the free and unplanned time in your day. When you subtract your future sleep from this number, you get an idea of the time you really have to work with. To make all of these numbers easier to track and understand, there are charts inside each column to help you visualize the numbers and statistics taken from your entries.
